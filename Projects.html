Data Quality Control for MRI files

MRI scans can help visualize what is going on inside someone's body, but all machines have some level of error. The goal of this project is to access the quality of scans based on various tags within a dicom file. Each manufacturer has it's own ranges of values which decide whether or not an scan is acceptable, so I had to determine the best way to organize a series of scans and then find ranges for each numeric tag section available.

Exploring Machine Learning on MRI files

The goal of this project was to determine if RandomForestClassifier could determine wheter or not the scan was of a specific part of the body. This required creating a method to label non-numeric data, separating a testing and training set, and testing accuracy of the model. Additionally, I applied a 3d plot to analyze if altering parameters resulting in higher accuracy.

Fingerprinting Project as an Undergraduate Researcher at UCSD's Medical School

I dedicated the summer of 2022 to research at the Majithia Lab which specializes in the Endocrinology system. Continuous glucose monitoring provides real time measurements of glucose levels for diabeteic patients in order to determine preventative care before spikes. My project was centered around determining whether or not indivdual patients had glucose traces unique to them. Ideally, after determinging values for various features on the glucose traces per day I would have applied machine learning techniques, however I was limited by the dataset. While some individuals had trends in their days, others had a significant amount of variation. To reach a more reliable conclusion, I believe I would need access to a dataset where patients had at least a month's worth of trace.
